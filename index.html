Charles H. Townes

American Physicist. (July 28, 1915 - January 27, 2015)

Charles H. Townes was an American physicist that worked on the theory and application of the maser, which he obtained the fundamental patent for.

Early Childhood & Personal Life.

Townes was born on July 28, 1915 in Greenville, South Carolina. It was here where Townes would grow up on Sumner Street in a home that would later be demolished to make space for the St. Francis Hospital. Charles Townes was the fourth child in his family which was a Baptist household that encouraged his intellectual pursuits. Throughout his childhood and adolescents Townes had attended public schools in which during the summers he would operate small street stands. In his you adult life Charles Townes decided to go to Furman University where he would study physics, math and biology. It was here where he would graduate Summa Cum Laude by the age of 19.

Accomplishments/Contributions Charles H. Townes Has Made to Society.

Charles H. Townes' has made a few contributions to society such as his theory of application of the maser, and other quantum electronics that were associated with both the laser and the maser which helped lead to the construction of oscillators and amplifier. As a result of these two inventions and his work in the field of quantum electronics, Townes was able to accomplish many things most specifically receiving a Nobel Prize in 1964 for physics.

Important Events That Shaped and Influenced Charles H. Townes.

During Townes' time at Columbia University, Townes had researched microwave physics, where he studied the interactions between microwaves and molecules, and the structure of molecules, atoms, and nuclei through the microwave spectra. As a result of his research Townes was able to conceive the idea for the maser, which helped lead to his notable invention of the maser and later the laser.

Hardships and Obstacles That Charles H. Townes Faced.

One of the obstacles that Townes had faced was that many of his colleagues at Columbia saw his projects as a waste of the government's money, and they believed that his microwave research projects and especially his maser project were impossible at worst and useless at best.

Interesting Facts About Charles H. Townes.

Charles H. Townes received the Templeton Prize for his efforts to build a bridge between science and religion. Along with this Townes received a Nobel Prize for physics in 1964 for his fundamental work in the field of quantum electronics, and graduated Summa Cum Laude at the age of 19 after his time at Furman University.
